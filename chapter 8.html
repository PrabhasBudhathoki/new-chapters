Chapter 8: Information Security and Cyber Law

This chapter covers the essential concepts of information security, including cybersecurity threats, protection mechanisms, and legal frameworks such as cyber laws.

Topics include ethical hacking, encryption, cybercrime, data privacy, and the legal aspects of cybersecurity.

Review Questions & Answers

1. What is information security?

Information security involves protecting digital data from unauthorized access, use, modification, or destruction.

2. What are the three main principles of information security?

The three principles are Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA Triad).

3. What is cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity refers to measures taken to protect computer systems, networks, and data from cyber threats.

4. What are common types of cyber threats?

Common threats include malware, phishing, hacking, ransomware, and denial-of-service attacks.

5. What is encryption?

Encryption is the process of converting data into a coded format to prevent unauthorized access.

6. What is the purpose of a firewall?

A firewall is a security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic.

7. What is ethical hacking?

Ethical hacking involves legally testing computer systems for vulnerabilities to improve security.

8. What are strong password practices?

Strong passwords should be long, complex, unique, and regularly updated.

9. What is two-factor authentication (2FA)?

2FA is an additional layer of security requiring two forms of verification to access an account.

10. What is phishing?

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information.

11. What are cyber laws?

Cyber laws are regulations that govern internet usage and address cybercrimes.

12. What is the Information Technology (IT) Act?

The IT Act is a law that regulates electronic transactions and cybercrimes.

13. What is identity theft?

14. What is spyware?

Spyware is malicious software that secretly collects user information.

15. What is ransomware?

Ransomware is malware that encrypts data and demands payment for decryption.

16. What is the role of an antivirus program?

Antivirus software detects and removes malware from a system.

17. What are digital signatures?

Digital signatures authenticate the identity of a sender and ensure message integrity.

18. What is GDPR?

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) is a data protection law that governs privacy rights.

19. What are the penalties for cybercrimes?

Penalties for cybercrimes include fines, imprisonment, or both, depending on the severity of the offense.

20. How can individuals protect themselves from cyber threats?

Individuals can use strong passwords, enable two-factor authentication, update software regularly, and avoid suspicious links.
